My Name is Tatsiana Kulakevich, and I am a PhD candidate in Political Science

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Political Science and an affiliate of the Center for European Studies at Rutgers University. My research focuses on international political economy, migration, and ethnic politics.

My dissertation examines the distribution of FDI and the role of immigration into the countries with weak rule of law. I show that while investors face risk when committing capital to productive use in the countries with weak rule of law, migrants, typically have a somewhat different risk profile when it comes to investing in their countries of origin.

My analyses appeared in media and academic journals, including The Monkey Cage - Washington Post, Belarus Digest, and East European Politics Societies and Cultures.

My specialties include Comparative Politics, International Relations, Eastern European politics, and Quantitative Methods.
